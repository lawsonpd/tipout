Frequently asked questions

How is my budget calculated?

Your daily budget is calculated by finding your average daily income (based on your average tips earned per day and your paychecks for the past 30 days) and substracting your daily expense cost (the total cost of your expenses divided by 30 days). Throughout the day, the budget will update to reflect your expenditures for the day.

If you're over or under budget with your spending, then your budget for the next 7 days will be adjusted to reflect this. So, if you're over budget, then your budget for the next 7 days will be reduced slightly to make up for the loss, and if you're under, then your budget for the next 7 days will be increased slightly since you then have more to spend.

What's the difference between an expense and an expenditure?

An expense
is a reoccuring bill or payment (e.g. rent, phone bill, car insurance, etc.)
An expenditure
is a one-off payment that isn't intended to happen regularly (e.g. lunch, dinner, coffee)

You may find that some things are more easily managed as expenses rather than expenditures. For example, if you buy the same bag of dog food each week, you could enter that as an expense, since you know you'll be buying it regularly.

On the other hand, even though you may go out to lunch everyday, you should probably enter that as an expenditure since the cost will likely be differnt each time.

What if I want to put money into savings?

We think this is a great idea. Presently, Tipout doesn't take into account money you have in savings or let you specify an amount of money you'd like to save each day or week.

Rest assured, however, that this is a feature we're actively developing and very soon you'll be able to keep track of savings as well.

Can I use Tipout if I don't earn tips?

Yes, you absolutely can! If you'd like to enter your income only as paychecks and ignore the tips section, then Tipout will calculate your budget based on just the paychecks.

Is there a Tipout app for Android or iOS?

For now, Tipout is just a web app (i.e. it runs in the browser). There are a couple of reasons for this.

  1. First, with a web app we're able to deploy changes and updates immediately. Deploying an app through the Google Play Store or Apple's App Store takes time and you would have to update your version of the app every time an update is made. We want to be able to react quickly to your feedback, so it's important that we can do that without the added inconvenience.
  2. Second, Tipout simply runs very fast in the browser. Native apps tend to be fast once they get going but are slow to start. You'll find that Tipout launches quickly since it doesn't have the overhead of a native app.

Why did it ask for my location when I signed up?

As we grow, we'd like to know where users are joining from! We're based in Nashville, Tennessee, but we want to help people from all across the country, so asking for your location is just a way to get to know you :)

(We won't share your information with any third-parties or use it for advertisements or promotions.)

How do I cancel my subscription?

You'll see the option to cancel if you select "Subscription" from the menu.
